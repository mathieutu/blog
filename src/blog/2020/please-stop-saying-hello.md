When using instant messaging like slack, discord, teams or whatever for working or in community group,
I often end up with situations I call "asking for asking" like this one following.

If I sent you the link to this page, please, read this carefully (and don't take that personally 😘!).

> _ME, WORKING ON SOMETHING..._
>
> - **_you_: Hi!**
> - **_me:_ Hello.**
>
> _ME, WAITING WHILE YOU'RE WRITING YOUR MESSAGE..._
>
> - **_you:_ How you're doing today?**
> - **_me:_ Good, and you?**
>
> _ME, WAITING WHILE YOU'RE WRITING YOUR MESSAGE..._
>
> - **_you:_ Fine, thanks!**
>
> _ME, WAITING WHILE YOU'RE WRITING YOUR MESSAGE..._
>
> - **_you:_ Do you have a minute? Can I bother you?**
> - **_me:_ Yes, what can I do for you?**
>
> _ME, WAITING WHILE YOU'RE WRITING YOUR MESSAGE..._
>
> - **_you:_ I'm working on [something] and I need [etc...]**
> - **_me:_ ok, so you can [answer...]**

<br>

So **PLEASE**, next time you find yourself saying “Hi” to someone on instant messaging, or asking if you may ask,
just hold back that impulse to hit Enter, take a new breathe, and just type your message right away.

Deal? 😉
